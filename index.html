About Me
    My grandfather, Avshalom Shohat, was born and raised in Haifa.

    Grandpa attended The Hebrew Reali School in Haifa and studied History and Political Science in Jerusalem.

    His father was one of the senior educators in the country and is the persona that influenced and inspired him the most throughout his life.

    He later moved to Calgary, Canada, and Michigan in the United States, where he worked as a teacher and studied Communication, and received his Doctor degree in Educational Technology.

    Upon his return to Israel, he worked in the Open University developing courses and a few years later in the College of Management as Head of the New Media Department.

    His hobbies are mainly exploring new gadgets of all sorts, growing fish in salted water in an aquarium, and of course, spending time with his family

    This year Grandpa celebrated his 80th birthday!!!

    Love you so much, grandpa ♥

Q & A


    Question 1
    Web1:
  • Limited interaction between sites and web users.
  • Simply web.
  • The content of the web pages was static - Read only web.

    Web2:
  • Interaction between web users and sites
  • Social Web - applications like Facebook.
  • Encourages information sharing, participation and collaboration - Read - Write web.

    Web3:
  • Semantic web which refers to the future.
  • Computers can interpret information like humans and intelligently generate and distribute useful content tailored to the needs of users.
  • Read - write - execute web.


    Question 2
    Use case: looking up a query- "How epilepsy affects driving?"

  1. Web 1.0- we will need to find or to know specific url to get the answer.
  2. Web 2.0- we can google it for example to find some relevant sites and links.
  3. Web 3.0- we can find a list of related answers using the semantic of the query and users posts about epilepsy and driving.
